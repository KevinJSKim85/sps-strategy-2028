## 4. Academic Strategy at SPS

### 4.1 Classical Honors Program (Most Important Program)

SPS has two "Next-Level Learning" special programs. One is the Applied Science and Engineering Program (ASEP), and the other is the Classical Honors Program.

What is the Classical Honors Diploma? It is a special diploma awarded only to students who complete advanced coursework in both Latin and Greek. Official SPS language: "one of the highest academic distinctions at St. Paul's School." This is a separate diploma from the standard one and is explicitly listed in college application materials.

Program requirements: Final grade of B+ or higher in Honors Latin + at least 2 terms of Ancient Greek with B+ or higher + application.

Why it matters:

- Official credential: "Classical Honors" is printed on the diploma -> immediately visible to admissions offices
- Activity opportunities: Join Classics Society, compete in Certamen (Latin quiz bowl), perform in annual Latin Play
- International travel: Biennial 2-week Italy or Greece travel program (March vacation)
- Graduation project: Complete a Capstone Project in 6th Form (research paper, performance, creative project, etc.)
- Scarcity: Very small elite cohort, typically only about 5-10 students per year

Execution plan:

- 10th grade (now): Start (or continue) Honors Latin. Maintaining B+ or above is essential.
- 11th grade: Start Ancient Greek (minimum 2 terms required). Take NLE Advanced Poetry. Take NGE Beginner Attic.
- 12th grade: Complete Capstone Project. Take top NLE level. Take NGE Intermediate+. Apply for Italy/Greece trip.

### 4.2 National Latin Exam (NLE) Progress Roadmap

NLE is a national exam held every year in February-March. There are currently 8 levels.

| Level | Typical Grade/Year | Award Tier | This Student's Strategy |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Introduction to Latin | Year 1 | Gold > Silver > Magna CL > Cum Laude | Already completed |
| Beginning Latin | Year 2 | Gold (top ~2%) > Silver > Magna CL | Already completed |
| Intermediate Latin | Year 3 | Same system | Already completed |
| Advanced Latin Prose | Year 4 | Gold cutoff: 32/40 (80%) | At this level in 10th grade now! Target Gold. |
| Advanced Latin Poetry | Year 5 | Interpreting Vergil, Horace, Ovid poetry | 11th grade target. Parallel with AP Latin-level rigor. |
| Advanced Reading Comp. | Year 6 | Highest level | 12th grade target. Gold here = scholarship eligibility. |

Scholarship note (very important): Maureen O'Donnell Scholarship -- awards $2,000 to up to 15 seniors nationwide who earn Gold in NLE Advanced Prose/Poetry/Reading Comprehension. Requires intent to study Latin or Greek in college. If this student earns Gold in Advanced Prose in 10th grade, eligibility is already established for senior year. This is a named national scholarship that can be listed on the Common App.

### 4.3 National Greek Exam (NGE) -- Add Greek

NGE is held annually in March. The number of national test-takers is very small, so award scarcity value is high.

| Level | National Test-Takers (2024-25) | Description | Recommendation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Beginning Attic Greek | 534 | Year 1 Attic Greek. Grammar and foundational reading. | Start here in 11th grade. Can run in parallel with Latin. |
| Intermediate Attic Greek | 247 | Prose reading: Xenophon, Plato, Lysias. | Take in 12th grade if progress aligns. |
| Attic Prose / Homeric Greek | 77-82 | Advanced: Homer, tragedy, speeches. | Stretch target for 12th grade. |

Key point: Only 534 students nationwide take Beginning Attic Greek. Compared with 107,000 NLE test-takers, that is a 200x difference. A Blue (Highest) award here is roughly within top ~40 nationwide -- an extremely strong scarcity signal for admissions.

### 4.4 Math Strategy

Recommended course: NetMath UIUC Computational Linear Algebra -- strongly recommended as dual enrollment next semester (11th grade). This is not a high-school "college-level" class; it is real university credit recorded on official UIUC transcript. An A is read by MIT, Stanford, and Caltech admissions as evidence of mathematical maturity.

Summer 2026 (after 10th grade):

- If admitted: If admitted to any of SUMAC, Ross, PROMYS, attend without exception. These programs have 5-15% admit rates and carry weight similar to USAMO qualification in college admissions. Ross is often considered the most rigorous in the math community, while SUMAC has the strongest brand visibility due to Stanford.
- If rejected by all, alternatives: Canada/USA Mathcamp (admit rate ~15-20%), HCSSiM (Hampshire College), Euler Circle (online, research track), or additional dual enrollment (Abstract Algebra, Real Analysis).

SPS internal math activities: Join Mathematics Society + participate in prize exams + participate in AMC/AIME. Take Advanced Topics (independent research) in 5th/6th Form. SPS offerings like Chaos Theory and Mathematical Problem Solving are also distinctive and usable as essay material.

Why is the math + classics combination powerful? Top universities such as Harvard, Princeton, Yale, Stanford, and MIT prefer applicants with broad intellectual range over applicants strong in only one field. A student who can read Vergil in original Latin and also solve competition math problems is a profile that pure STEM applicant pools almost never produce. Princeton has a real example of a Classics + CS student selected as Latin Salutatorian (2017).
