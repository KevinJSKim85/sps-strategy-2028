## 5. SPS Extracurricular & Leadership Strategy

SPS has over 70 clubs and an independent leadership structure. Below are priority activities aligned with this student's profile.

### 5.1 Leadership Position Roadmap (by Grade)

| Grade | Target Positions | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 4th Form (10th grade) | Form Representative (2 elected per form)<br>Club officer / founder<br>Chapel Council member | Start leadership early. Form Rep participates in Student Council and is directly involved in school operations. |
| 5th Form (11th grade) | Club President (Classics Society, Math Society, Debate)<br>Chapel Warden or Chapel Prefect<br>Varsity team Captain | 11th grade is the core leadership year in practice. Club president + Chapel role is the ideal combination. |
| 6th Form (12th grade) | Sixth Form President (top student office -- election)<br>Sixth Form Officer (VP, Secretary, Treasurer)<br>Prefect (dorm mentor)<br>Student Mosaic Council | Sixth Form Officers meet weekly with the Rector (head of school). Directly referenced in school recommendations. |

### 5.2 Must-Do Activities (by Priority)

Priority 1 (directly supports profile):

- Classics Society: Join immediately. Required for the Classical Honors Diploma. Participate in Certamen (Latin quiz bowl) + Latin Play.
- Mathematics Society: Join. Participate in prize exams and peer tutoring. Participate in AMC/AIME via SPS.
- Debate Team: SPS has a team. The combination of classics (rhetoric) background + debate is strong.
- Missionary Society (revive with AI): This is SPS's local service club, but current activity is minimal. Revive it through an AI project. Start development in summer -> launch immediately in 11th grade. Example: AI-based map dashboard visualizing local community issues, or AI-enabled support campaigns for local residents. -> Precisely fits Section 2 "visible impact" principle.

Priority 2 (profile expansion):

- Chapel Talk: SPS tradition where students speak before the whole school. A highly impressive public-speaking experience and strong essay material. Publicly archived on the SPS website as "Heard in Chapel."
- The Pelican (school newspaper) or Horae Scholasticae (literary magazine): SPS's oldest publications. Editor-in-Chief experience is direct evidence of writing ability.
- Asian/Korean Society (choose one): Pick one of Asian Society or Korean Society where officer probability is higher. Doing both splits time. Focus on where President/VP is realistic.
- Orchestra (violin -- supporting role): Keep orchestra violin with minimal time investment. Serve as a supporting role. Occupies one EC line on the application.

### 5.3 ASEP Application (Urgent!)

Applied Science and Engineering Program (ASEP) is the most selective academic program at SPS.

- Selection size: Only 12 students selected from 5th Form each year
- Summer research: Summer research internship in university/research labs (Mass General Hospital, Northeastern, etc.)
- Graduation project: 6th Form Capstone Project + poster presentation
- Application timing: Must apply in 4th Form (10th grade). This is the application window now.

Strategic meaning: Completing both the Classical Honors Diploma and ASEP is a very rare combination in SPS history. Graduates with both become immediately noticeable profiles for MIT, Harvard, and Princeton admissions.

### 5.4 SPS-Unique Opportunities

- No AP classes? SPS does not offer AP coursework. Instead, it runs its own Honors/Advanced course system. Do not worry -- admissions offices understand SPS well, and SPS College Advising Office (Dean Greg Buckles, Director Erin Ainor) explains this through the school profile.
- Harkness method: Classes are conducted at Harkness Tables (oval discussion tables). This is very distinctive material when explaining "my learning environment" in college essays.
- 100% boarding: SPS is one of very few 100% boarding schools in the U.S. Teachers as well as students live on campus. This leads to deep teacher relationships -> stronger recommendation letters.
- Independent Study idea: In 6th Form, run Capstone on a math+classics fusion topic. Example: "Mathematical structure of ancient Greek architecture" or "Statistical analysis of meter in Latin poetry." This type of project is read as evidence of academic independence and interdisciplinary thinking.
