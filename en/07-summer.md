## 7. Summer Program Strategy (Math-Centered)

### Summer 2026 (After 10th Grade)

| Program | Admit Rate | Signal Strength | Current Status | Description |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| SUMAC (Stanford) | ~5-8% | Top-tier | Applied | Pure math. Stanford brand. 4-week residential. |
| Ross (Ohio State) | ~15% | Top-tier | Applied | Number theory. Proof-based. 6 weeks. Considered one of the most rigorous in math community. |
| PROMYS (Boston Univ) | ~10-15% | Very strong | Applied | Number theory. 6 weeks. Can return as JC (Junior Counselor) next year. |
| NetMath UIUC | Open enrollment | Strong (dual enrollment) | Recommended | Computational Linear Algebra. University credit. Recommended during 11th-grade semesters. |

If admitted: attend without exception. Admission to these programs carries weight comparable to USAMO qualification in college admissions.

If rejected by all (do not be discouraged):

- Priority 1: Apply to Canada/USA Mathcamp (~15-20% admit rate). If not yet applied, do it now.
- Priority 2: HCSSiM (Hampshire College). Small cohort (about 50). Proof-based.
- Priority 3: Euler Circle (online, research track). Can run in parallel with other programs.
- Priority 4: Additional NetMath UIUC coursework (Abstract Algebra or Real Analysis). An A grade = strong signal.

### Summer 2027 (After 11th Grade)

This summer is not for starting new activities; it is for deepening existing ones.

- If already experienced in math programs: Return to Ross/PROMYS as Junior Counselor -- more impressive than first-year participation.
- Otherwise: Independent math research (target paper writing), or university Classics summer intensive.
- Important: After this summer, application season begins. Activity list should be fully complete by August.
