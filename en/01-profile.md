## 1. Student Profile Summary

This strategy was prepared for a student with the profile below.

- Nationality: Korean national, U.S. permanent resident (Permanent Resident / Green Card)
- School: St. Paul's School (SPS), Concord, NH. Enrolled in 4th-6th Form (10th-12th grade). U.S. 3-year boarding school track.
- Mathematics: Strong in math. Applications completed for SUMAC (Stanford), Ross (Ohio State), and PROMYS (Boston Univ). NetMath UIUC Computational Linear Algebra dual enrollment is recommended for next semester.
- Classics: Completed National Latin Exam (NLE) through Advanced Prose level (10th grade). Plan to continue NLE in 11th grade and add National Greek Exam (NGE).
- Athletics: Republic of Korea U17 national alpine ski team member. On SPS varsity ski team, aiming for captain (realistic possibility). Participates in full pre-season training camp pool.
- Goal: Admission to a U.S. Top 20 university. Intends to apply as a Classical Studies major. The core narrative is contribution to Classics through AI.

Profile analysis: The combination of math olympiad track, classical languages (Latin/Greek), and national-team skiing is statistically very rare in the Top 20 applicant pool. This document distinguishes schools where this combination is truly effective (Harvard, Dartmouth, Princeton, etc.) and schools where it is less effective (Caltech, Carnegie Mellon, etc.), and provides school-specific guidance on what to emphasize.
